Lionel Messi

Argentine forward with record eight Ballon d'Or awards, World Cup winner, and Barcelona legend known for incredible dribbling and playmaking.

Cristiano Ronaldo

Portuguese superstar with five Ballon d'Or awards, known for incredible athleticism, goal-scoring records, and success across multiple leagues.

Pelé

Brazilian legend and three-time World Cup winner, regarded as one of the greatest footballers in history with over 1,000 career goals.

Diego Maradona

Argentine magician who led his country to 1986 World Cup glory, famous for exceptional dribbling skills and the "Hand of God" goal.

Johan Cruyff

Dutch master who revolutionized football with "Total Football" philosophy, three-time Ballon d'Or winner and influential coach.

Zinedine Zidane

French maestro known for elegance and technical brilliance, World Cup and Champions League winner, legendary Real Madrid player.

Ronaldo Nazário

Brazilian phenomenon known as "The Original Ronaldo," two-time World Cup winner and one of the most complete strikers in history.

Alfredo Di Stéfano

Argentine-Spanish legend who dominated European football with Real Madrid, winning five consecutive European Cups in the 1950s.
